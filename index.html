Antiobiotic sensitivity Test - Simulation

The Antibiotic sensitivity test (also called Kirby-Bauer disk diffusion test) is used to determine which antibiotics are effective against a specific strain of bacteria. The bacteria are spread across a petri dish and antibiotic discs are placed on the surface, then the dish is incubated to allow bacterial growth. If an antibiotic is effective, it prevents the bacteria from growing nearby, creating a clear circular area called the zone of inhibition (or zone of sensitivity). The larger the clear zone, the more sensitive the bacteria are to that antibiotic.

To use this simulation, select the bacterial strain to use. Then choose one or more antibiotics and drag them onto the dish. Then, press the Incubate button to begin the growth process. Watch carefully as the bacteria spread across the plate. Clear circular zones will appear around antibiotics that successfully inhibit bacterial growth. Compare the sizes of these zones to determine which antibiotic works best against the strain.
